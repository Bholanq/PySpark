### What is Big Data?
	Big data is essentially huge amounts of data that is so large, fast, complex that is cannot be handled by traditional databases and tools. Not only the size and quantity but also how fast its coming in, variety and how useful it is.

### 3V's of Big Data
- Velocity - The rate at which the data is coming in.
- Volume - How much data in coming in.
- Varity - What different types of data.

### Dealing with Big Data
1. **Monolithic Architecture (Vertical Scaling)**
	- ![[Pasted image 20260303152140.png|351]]
	- **Vertical scaling** means increasing the capacity of a **single machine/server** to handle more load. - Server(RAM+, Memory+, etc.) to make it better at handling higher volumes of data. 
	- ##### Problem - Single Point of failure, - Hardware limit (you can't upgrade infinitely)
	
2. **Distributed Architecture (Horizontal Scaling)**
	 - **Horizontal scaling** means increasing system capacity by **adding more machines/servers** instead of upgrading a single one.
